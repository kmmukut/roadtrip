5-Day Southwest Parks Road Trip

This itinerary is a fast-paced "greatest hits" tour, focusing on iconic viewpoints and short walks. It's perfect for travelers who want to see as much as possible in a limited time and are prepared for significant driving. With Hildale as your central base, you'll be making long day trips in a large loop through Utah and Arizona.

Day 1: The Grand Arrival (Thursday, Sept 18)

  • Route: Las Vegas → Grand Canyon South Rim → Hildale, UT
  • What to Expect: A classic travel day that starts in the city and ends at one of the world's greatest natural wonders. The main event is the Grand Canyon, where you'll hop between several easily accessible viewpoints, each offering a staggering perspective of its immense scale. Expect a long day with a late arrival at your base in Hildale.
  • Total Driving: ~8 hours

Day 2: Iconic Western Landscapes (Friday, Sept 19)

  • Route: Hildale → Page, AZ → Monument Valley → Hildale, UT
  • What to Expect: This is a journey into the heart of the American West. You'll experience the dramatic 1,000-foot drop at Horseshoe Bend and then drive into the landscape of classic Western films at Monument Valley, with its world-famous towering sandstone buttes.
  • Total Driving: ~8 hours

Day 3: A Tale of Two Parks (Saturday, Sept 20)

  • Route: Hildale → Canyonlands NP → Arches NP → Hildale, UT
  • What to Expect: Your longest day of driving is rewarded with visits to two of Utah's "Mighty 5." In the morning, you'll gaze out over the vast, layered canyons from the "Island in the Sky" district of Canyonlands. In the afternoon (per your timed entry!), you'll explore the otherworldly landscape of Arches, home to the highest concentration of natural stone arches on Earth.

Day 4: Canyons & Hoodoos (Sunday, Sept 21)

  • Route: Hildale → Zion NP → Bryce Canyon NP → Hildale, UT
  • What to Expect: A day of incredible geological diversity. You'll start with a scenic drive through Zion, dwarfed by its massive sandstone cliffs. Then, you'll travel to the high-altitude wonderland of Bryce Canyon, filled with thousands of whimsical, spire-shaped rock formations called "hoodoos." Staying for sunset is a must, as the fading light makes the orange rock glow.
  • Total Driving: ~6 hours

Day 5: One Last Wonder (Monday, Sept 22)

  • Route: Hildale → Coral Pink Sand Dunes → Las Vegas
  • What to Expect: A relaxed final morning exploring a unique and beautiful landscape. The iron oxides in the sand give these dunes a stunning color. It's the perfect, low-key stop to stretch your legs before the final drive back to Las Vegas to catch your flight.
  • Total Driving: ~3.5 hours
